Exhibition Narratives

Two curated journeys through Austrian art. Choose a path and let the artworks tell a story - either in the order history unfolded, or through the thread of emotional expression that connects centuries.

Timeline Narrative

🖼 18 artworks ⏱ 60–90 min 📅 c. 1770 → c. 1920

A chronological journey through 150 years of Austrian artistic development. From Messerschmidt's Enlightenment-era Character Heads through Biedermeier domestic intimacy, the atmospheric Stimmungsmalerei of Schindler and Blau, the ornamental gold of Klimt's Secession, and finally the raw expressionist line of Schiele and Kokoschka.

Suitable for all audiences. Description mode can be changed freely at any artwork.

Start Timeline Narrative →

Emotion & Expression

🖼 9 artworks ⏱ 30–45 min 🎭 Thematic path

A thematic pathway that cuts across chronology to follow a single thread: the body and face as sites of psychological revelation. From Messerschmidt's grimacing heads to Romako's trembling admiral, Klimt's femme fatale, and Schiele's anguished projections - this narrative explores how Austrian artists made inner states visible.

Especially suited to visitors interested in psychology, theatre, or modern subjectivity.

How to Use the Narratives

Once inside a narrative, a progress banner shows your position in the sequence. Use the buttons - or your keyboard arrow keys - to move through the artworks. You can adjust the description mode (length, level, tone) at any point without leaving the narrative. Your position is always saved in the URL so you can bookmark or share any artwork within any narrative.

1

Choose a narrative above and click the Start button to enter at the first artwork.

2

Navigate with Next / Previous buttons, or use ← → arrow keys on your keyboard.

3

Adjust descriptions at any artwork - Brief, Standard, or Full; Intro, General, or Scholar level.

Prefer browsing freely? Visit the Exhibition Map to explore by room, or view all artworks in chronological order on the Timeline.
